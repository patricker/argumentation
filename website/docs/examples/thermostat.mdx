> Two roommates argue about the thermostat. Alice wants 72°F; Bob wants 68°F. We model the disagreement as a single scene with two arguments and a weighted attack, then watch how the outcome changes as we move β — the scene's intensity dial.

A worked example for the `encounter-argumentation` bridge: a scene where both parties have a valid argument, the arguments attack each other, and the framework decides the outcome based on β.

## Drag β to see the scene flip

<BetaPlayground
  title="Thermostat scene — live"
  args={[
    {id: 'A', label: 'Alice: warmer is healthier'},
    {id: 'B', label: 'Bob: cooler saves energy'},
  ]}
  attacks={[{from: 'B', to: 'A', weight: 0.4}]}
  initialBeta={0}
/>

Two arguments, one directed weighted attack. The attack weight is **0.4**, meaning Bob's argument is credible enough to undermine Alice's when β is at or below 0.4. The slider above runs the real `argumentation` engine compiled to WebAssembly — every drag recomputes credulous acceptance in your browser.

## What's happening

At **β = 0**, every attack binds. Alice's argument has a live attacker, so it is not credulously accepted; her action is rejected. She falls back to a non-contentious action.

At **β = 0.4**, the 0.4-weight attack becomes droppable (the inclusive boundary — see [β as scene intensity](/concepts/weighted-and-beta)). Alice's argument becomes credulously accepted; the `StateActionScorer` boosts it; she picks it.

At **β = 0.5** and **β = 1.0**, the dynamic is the same as at 0.4 for this fixture — no additional edges bind differently past the 0.4 threshold.

## The trace

Each beat is recorded in the trace JSON (for example, `/traces/thermostat-b05.json`). You can read off the seeded arguments, the attack graph, the per-beat acceptance decision, and any errors latched by the bridge. Replayable and deterministic.

## Reading the outcome

In this scene, the outcome is a function of β rather than a branching tree you hand-author. Turn β down for courtroom-sharp scenes where every objection lands; turn β up for family-dinner scenes where people let each other have a point.
